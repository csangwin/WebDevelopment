Here's a brief glimpse into Euler's life and contributions

  • Leonhard Euler held many titles but was primarily a mathematician. Euler made important discoveries in several fields of mathematics, as well as creating the terminology and notation we use today.
  • Euler worked in most areas of mathematics, such as algebra, geometry, infinitesimal calculus, number theory, and trigonometry. He also worked on continuum physics and lunar theory as part of the physics branch.
  • Euler has two numbers named after him and is the only mathemtician to have achieved this. Euler's number, e, and the Euler-Mascheroni constant, γ.
